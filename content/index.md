# Welcome to My OpenClaw Blog

This blog is a running diary of my experiences with [OpenClaw](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw) — an open-source AI proxy framework that lets you route AI requests across multiple providers, models, and channels.

I'm Dillon Coetzer, manager of [Ladysmith Wireless Solutions](https://lws.splynx.online), a WISP in Ladysmith, KwaZulu-Natal. I run OpenClaw on a Mac Mini in my office, connected to Telegram, Home Assistant, Splynx, and a handful of other systems that keep the lights on.

## What You'll Find Here

- **OpenClaw version notes** — what works, what breaks, what I wish I'd known sooner
- **LLM comparisons** — MiniMax vs DeepSeek vs Sonnet vs Qwen, tested in real automation scenarios
- **Plugin discoveries** — the skills, scripts, and workflows that actually make a difference
- **Automation wins (and fails)** — voltage monitoring, tower alerts, Respond.io integration, and more
- **ISP operations** — the boring-but-important stuff a WISP manager deals with daily

## Why a Public Blog?

Mostly for myself — writing things down forces me to think clearly about what I've learned. If it helps someone else who's treading the same ground, that's a bonus.
